Research

I had conducted not only background research but also key insights into the World Vision organisation, a look into user research with interviews and questionnaires to properly gain better information and awareness of the different user groups that potentially use the World Vision website and had also done proper and informed user profiles following the vast information gained from my user research.

Organisation Description:

"World Vision is an Australian charity organisation that focuses towards poverty and injustice within children, families and communities - acting as a global movement in supporting the world. They are particularly focused on providing short and long term assistance towards vulnerable children and families living in poverty. World Vision uses fundraising and charity efforts in support of their work and overall aim to end poverty."

Key Information and Statistics:

Introduction Description:

The key insights found from the World Vision's website not only gave proper understanding and legitimacy to their cause but it was also later used in the development of my proposal website - whereby I used these key statistics and information to be put and showcased in a better and more visually aesthethic manner for newcomers who do not know about World Vision for my proposed website.

List of Key Insights:

  • Overall mission is to eliminate poverty and its causes however they are also more child-orientated or focused, working on their wellbeing

  • Aims to also bring "hope and opportunities to vulnerable children and communitiies"

  • 3.4 million people gained access to clean water through World Vision's work

  • World Vision and their involved community have impacted the lives of over 200 million vulnerable children - tackling the root of poverty

  • Spans nearly 100 countries, 37,000 staff and raised more than $2.9 billion in 2019

  • 81.7% of their donated funds are used for field programs and advocacy work - helping childrens/families. 11.3% of their work also focuses on fundarising and 7% goes to administration

  • In 2019, $28.7 million in appeals, donations and gifts were received and used by World Vision (World Vision, 2019)

User Research:

Identification of User Groups:

In order to identify and research the users for the World Vision website - an observation and look into the World Vision's social media sites were conducted alongside an article that was used to backup the assumptions of age groups - detailing the demographics on Facebook and Instagram ((Jackson, 2020) showing how Instagram has more younger age groups whilst Facebook has more diverse age groups, with users spanning from young to old - however more older audiences were found on Facebook, whilst on Instagram younger age groups were typically found. As a result of this three user groups were identified:

  • 18 - 24 age group (teenagers)
  • 25-50 age group (adults)
  • 60-85 age group (elders)

Secondary Research:

These three main groups were then accompanied by secondary research in the form of a 2019 Australian report (CAF, 2019) giving useful and insightful statistics such as "Those aged 25-34 are the most likely to have made a donation in the last 12 months (74%)." and "For older donors, caring about the cause (64% of those aged 55 years and over vs. 54% average) and trusting the organisations they donate to (43% vs. 32%) are more common reasons to give. (CAF, 2019)" to detail the behaviours, motivations and requirements for mainly the adults and elders age groups.

Primary Research:

This was then followed by more primary research done in the form of informal interviews with 4 peers around the teenagers age group to gain insights, understanding and motivations within that group as well as creating a survey/questionnaire having meaningful open-ended questions that was distributed on Reddit to appeal to all age groups (being able to gain 10 responses from the Google Form survey/questionnaire I made), providing better understanding and refinement to the motivations, needs/requirements and frustrations experienced by the World Vision donation section of the website in specific as my concept proposal focused on developing on the donation section in specific (more details can be seen in "The Concept" subheading!")

Target Audience for Website Proposal

Given the exploration of my 3 user profiles/personas - it is most suitable that my target audience is aimed towards young - middle-aged adults (20-35) given how they have expendable income for donating which is the main concept of my website proposal alongside having good knowledge and wisdom on the topic.

The 2019 Australian report (CAF, 2019) also states that “Those aged 25-34 are the most likely to have made a donation in the last 12 months (74%). (CAF, 2019)” - meaning that it’ll make it more likely they would donate to a charity as well known as World Vision.

They would also be knowledgeable on technology/be tech-savvy, giving more freedom for my website design, in exploring different styles and structures - rather than being limited to a really simplistic design had it been targeted to older or younger demographics who are not as well known in technology nor as tech-savvy.

The Concept

The proposed concept idea for my website is to design a data-driven or statistic-driven visualization for my website - showing appropriate and engaging statistics in support of the cause of donating for the World Vision charity. It will provide some key insight towards the organisation’s aim/work, the organisation's positive impact and the benefits they bring in relation to their cause and the website will also follow through with a person’s impact of donating to the cause with corresponding statistics, to visually show the donation impact.

NOTE: Given the limitations and my lack on expertise of Javascript - the data visualisation and statistics will be made in Illustrator and used on the website - having unique CSS animations tthat will pop-in or come from the side - in an effort to not make it not too static and mundane.

Home Landing Wireframe

This wireframe aligned to the final decision I had chosen from my landing page sketch - using an image carousel with text in front or within boxes later on - to demonstrate the cause and mission of World Vision in a quick, succinct and efficient manner. It also has a little arrow on the bottom to indicate the user to scroll down or click on the arrow to go to the next section - which is the "Our Impact" section.

I also considered and will use this phone resolution across my different pages and so when it comes to the coding/implementation stage - acting as a good representation on how I would fit my webpages onto a phone device. Our Impact Wireframe

In this section - I used two pages to directly demonstrate the impact World Vision has towards their cause via the first page being a world map display with infographics (made in Illustrator) and the side bar showing text about how World Vision works with 100 worlds globally. The 2nd page as a user slides down again - shows additional statistics and information towards their impact once again, demonstrated via boxes and text next to it. It then directs the person to the next section, "Your Impact" after knowing what World Vision can do, as seen on the bottom of the page




Your Impact Wireframe

This section is the most elaborate and extensive in terms of data/statistical information - using images, infographic elements and text to demonstrate what the impact of the user's donation is to World's vision cause of poverty and injustice with the 2nd panel or page adding as an additional bonus to convince the user in understanding that World Vision does make use of a person's donation funds in a globally impactful and beneficial way with the statistics to prove it. This would encourage the user to donate and slide down to do so - leading to the final section, "Donate Now".



Donate Now Wireframe

This final and important section - is where the user can vote for the World Vision charity - being given an option to choose the cause, deciding on how the user is going to pay (once or monthly) and choosing the amount they want. The impact of donating a specific amount (for instance: $15) is then emphasised by the right panel - whereby infographics with the amount by the side, demonstrate the impact a person brings when they donate. An example would be, donating $15 would allow for the better development of water facilities around the world (NOTE: this is an example! Not an actual statistic). There will also be a donate button as well (which is seen in the "Mockups" panel below!) which I had not included to make space and show the necessary details.



Mockups

Mockups were the final step conducted - whereby visual aesthethics and colour were added to my wireframes to stylistically experiment and represent the website I'm intending to create - with appropriate images and elements being more visible and easy to notice. It was also during stage that I developed the required and only main images and data/statistics needed for my mockups within Adobe XD like my wireframes.

NOTE: Some of the images used (example: the World Map image) are a temporary placement from online (and citated in references area) and are just used to ensure or see how the image fits or looks before creating some of the my heavy images/assets. Home Landing Mockup

The landing home page mockup is a simplistic image carousel that slides across when the person clicks on the right arrow or on the next concurrent bubble (after the grey one). I was also able to create a nice navigation bar that would be easy to use and conveys the "Donate Now" button in a clear and persuading manner - being coloured in green. Going down leads to the next page - "Our Impact" being clearly indicated on the bottom of the page.

The use of grey as the background, tinges of orange for the top and bottom of the pages and overall black/white for textual and graphical elements were also visual aesthethics used - being simplistic and not too overbearing, allowing for a smooth flowing and consistent layout and style.

I also consciously made the bottom colours become a brighter orange overtime (from the home landing page being soft orange tinge to a bright orange by the end of the sections (at the donation section) to subtly demonstrate progression of the pages as a user scrolls down, acting as a neat and nifty feature, taking the colour shading inspiration from my wireframes.

A phone mockup was also made - showing similar form with my wireframe - but incorporating colour to accurately lead the user's eyes to where they should look (e.g bottom of page to scroll down and scroll bar to indicate sliding images) and the sliding bar being moved relative to the text to make for a structurally aesthethic landing page



Our Impact Mockup

This section of the mockups focuses on portraying a world map - that shows the areas World Vision operates in and will have infographic elements within the image, alongside more information and statistical data - shown in the form of images and text, as the person slides down from the World Map section, allowing them to see the impact World Vision brings - being more emphasised also in the "Your Impact" section which follows after





Your Impact Mockup

This mockup section contains the most data, statistics and information to emphasise the benefits that comes along with donating to World Vision - with statistics such as "72% of our funds come from the Australian community" helping to elaborately convey and show the actions World Vision brings and does for the world, when the user donates to them. This once again - legitimises and helps my target audience of 20-35 year olds, to gain a sense of trust for them, and as a result become inclined to click on the "Donate Now" button on the bottom of the page after knowing all the full details and information.





Donate Now Mockup

The final mockup section - visually portrays how a person can select a cause (which is water in this example and will contain of water, education and general donation in the final design), select the amount they want/customise the amount they want (via a custom input) and also see the impact of donating different amounts ($25, $50, $100) on the right handside - which shows a carousel slider of the benefits of donating for the cause they choose. I also decided to remove the type of payment they want (once, monthly) - because it would be too much work to do and code - as the World Vision website shows different benefits for donating once and donating monthly.
